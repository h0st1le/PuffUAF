Fall Dynamics Models

There are three different models that describe how ash particles fall due to gravity
  • constant - Assumes laminar flow and a constant air viscosity.
  • stokes - Assumes laminar flow, but computes a local viscosity as a function of air temperature
  • reynolds - Computes the local Reynolds number in order to determine whether the flow is laminar, turbulent, or transitional. Local viscosity is computed as a function of temperature.

Run time for stokes or reynolds is longer because the local conditions must be computed for each particle at every time step.
